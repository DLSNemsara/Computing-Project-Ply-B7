#Welcome To Horton Plains

Why You Should Visit Horton Plains National Park!

Horton Plains National Park is a national park in the central highlands of Sri Lanka that was designated in 1988. It is located at an elevation of 2,100–2,300 m (6,900–7,500 ft) and encompasses montane grassland and cloud forest. It is rich in biodiversity and many species found here are endemic to the region. It is also a popular tourist destination and is situated 8 kilometres from Ohiya, 6 kilometres from the world-famous Ohiya Gap/Dondra Watch and 32 kilometres from Nuwara Eliya.
The sheer precipice of World's End and Baker's Falls are among the tourist attractions of the park.

Car Parking

Animal Photos
Capture photos of various animals and most of them are endemic species in Sri Lanka.
Guide Services
Our team is well experience and expertise in nature trekking tours in Horton Plains national park. And our guides are well knowledgeable about Horton Plains national park and natural habitats of animals.
Food & Beverages
Small cafeteria that located near to the National Park's ticket office. And you can get refreshments there while enjoying the scenery.
Water Falls & Great World's End Drop
Baker's fall which is 22m in height is a beautiful waterfall in Horton Plains. And Great World's End Drop is a sheer cliff, with a drop of about 4,000 feet. It is one of the most visited parts of the Park
